Location details

We'll be celebrating at En Public in Griftpark. It's easy to reach from the city and around 15 minutes from Utrecht Centraal. The space gives us the best of both: a lovely terrace outside and a comfortable indoor area, so we'll be able to enjoy the evening whatever the weather.

What to wear

The overall style of the celebration is cocktail attire — elegant, relaxed, and festive, without being too formal. Think polished and occasion-ready, but not black tie.

Cocktail dresses
Soft, elegant, celebration-ready
We'd love to see you in cocktail-style outfits, ideally dresses. Soft, elegant, and celebration-ready looks will fit the mood beautifully. Please avoid white and black.

Saturday picnic

If you'd like to keep the celebration going, we'd love to see you again the next day for a relaxed afternoon outdoors. We'll meet at 16:30 in Wilhelminapark for a casual BBQ or picnic, fresh air, games, and some easy time together after the wedding day.

It will be informal and open to everyone — we'd be very happy if you join us. Please bring some snacks, drinks, blankets, and a good mood.

Good to know

Getting there +
The venue is about 15 minutes from Utrecht Centraal, and the directions button above will make it easy to find on the day.
Rain or shine +
We're ready for all kinds of June weather. There will be both outdoor terrace space and a cozy indoor area, so we'll be comfortable whatever the forecast brings.
Creative surprises +
If you'd like to prepare a song, dance, toast, game, or any other small creative gesture, we'd absolutely love that. Nothing is expected, but all ideas are very welcome — just let us know in advance so we can make space for it in the evening.
A note on gifts +
Your presence at our wedding is already the most important gift. If you'd still like to give something, a contribution towards our future together would mean a great deal to us. And if you feel inspired to bring something small and personal, we'd love that too — especially if it carries a bit of your heart, humour, or creativity.
Do I need to prepare a toast? +
Absolutely. This is a Russian-influenced celebration, so a heartfelt toast, funny story, song, dance, or "just one quick speech" that somehow becomes three is basically part of the dress code.
Will there be Russian wedding chaos? +
Only the good kind. Expect warmth, laughter, enthusiastic toasts, and at least one moment where nobody is fully sure what is happening — but everyone is enjoying it.
